# Product

## Register
product

## Users
Cleared serves compliance review teams working on customer-facing communications before they ship.

- Content authors submit drafts, need fast quote-level feedback, and resubmit fixed versions.
- Compliance officers triage failed or uncertain documents, accept or dismiss findings, and record final decisions.
- Compliance leads maintain the rubric, run the golden-set gate, and monitor review quality.
- Auditors inspect complete document history and export review records.

## Product Purpose
Cleared is an AI-assisted compliance review web app for emails, letters, landing-page copy, and other customer-facing documents. It exists to shorten the first review loop while preserving human authority and a reliable audit trail.

Success means users can see what needs attention, why it matters, which exact source text triggered the finding, who made the final call, and which rubric version governed the review.

## Brand Personality
Calm, exacting, and institutional. The interface should feel like an internal tool at a well-run regulated firm: quiet enough for daily work, precise enough for risk decisions, and polished enough to trust with audit records.

## Anti-references
- Generic startup dashboards with decorative gradients, oversized metric cards, and vague product claims.
- AI review tools that hide source evidence behind scores or summaries.
- Compliance software that is visually dense but careless about hierarchy, legibility, and decision provenance.
- Marketing pages that explain the category without showing the actual product.
- Novel controls, decorative motion, or invented affordances that slow down standard review work.

## Design Principles
- Evidence before opinion: reviews lead with quotes, criteria, rubric version, and decision history.
- Humans stay final: AI can route and explain, but officers make the binding decision.
- Dense, not crowded: operational screens should reveal many records while keeping hierarchy calm and scan-friendly.
- One vocabulary everywhere: buttons, tables, states, badges, forms, and panels should behave consistently across every role.
- Demo honestly: when storage or reviewers are in demo mode, surface that plainly without alarming the user.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA contrast for text and interactive states. Preserve keyboard navigation, visible focus, reduced-motion support, semantic tables/forms, and readable layouts down to narrow mobile widths. Do not rely on color alone for verdicts, severities, or decisions.
